# Avatar: Players

_Read by: all agents. The adult or teen player buying gear for themselves._

## Snapshot

_An active player. Rec league, adult men's or women's league, college player, high school player, semi-pro. Knows the sport and the gear. Will not be talked down to._

## Who They Are

- **Age range:** 14 to 45.
- **Playing level:** ranges from pickup once a week to high school varsity to adult amateur to small-time semi-pro.
- **Position awareness:** they know their position and often buy gear for it (keepers buy gloves, forwards argue about cleat weight, CBs care about durability).
- **Budget:** wide range, but they will spend $200+ on a boot they trust.

## What They Actually Need

- Cleats by surface: FG, AG, SG, turf, indoor.
- Keeper gloves by cut (negative, roll finger, flat palm, hybrid).
- Quality socks, guards, base layers.
- Training equipment for solo work (rebounder, cones, ball).
- Restocks of the same model they already like.

## Pain Points

- Their favorite cleat model got discontinued.
- Half-size issue between brands.
- Surface mismatch (wearing FG on turf ruins studs).
- Guards that rotate or slide.
- Keeper gloves that tear in five sessions.

## How They Search

- "[cleat model] wide feet review"
- "best turf shoes 2026"
- "negative cut keeper gloves"
- "Tiempo vs Morelia"
- "indoor soccer shoes hard court"
- "cleats for flat feet"

## Content That Would Land With Them

- Honest cleat comparisons written by someone who has worn both.
- "When to replace your cleats."
- Keeper glove buying guides by cut.
- Position-specific gear guides.
- Pro player gear breakdowns.

## What They Do NOT Care About

- Fluff.
- Generic roundups that clearly were not tested.
- Marketing copy on the product page.

## Who They Trust

- Unisport, SoccerBible, Weston, and other YouTube cleat reviewers.
- Teammates.
- Their own history with a brand.
