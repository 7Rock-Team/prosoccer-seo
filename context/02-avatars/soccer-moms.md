# Avatar: Soccer Moms (and Soccer Dads)

_Read by: all agents. This is likely ProSoccer's largest organic traffic segment. Get this one right._

## Snapshot

_The parent of a youth soccer player. Shops for cleats, shin guards, socks, balls, and jerseys. Tends to Google questions before buying and wants to be told what to get, not pitched._

## Who They Are

- **Age range:** typically 30 to 50.
- **Kids ages:** 5 to 17 covers the range; U6 to U14 is the sweet spot.
- **Soccer background:** mixed. Some played, many did not. Will not pretend to know gear.
- **Budget:** varies wildly. Rec league parents spend $40 to $80 per cleat; club parents spend $150 to $300.
- **Decision trigger:** growing feet, lost gear, start of season.

## What They Actually Need

- Accurate sizing guidance (youth cleat sizing is genuinely confusing).
- Honest guidance on when to upgrade from entry-level to mid-tier.
- Shin guard sizing.
- Ball size by age.
- What to bring to the first practice.

## Pain Points

- "Is this the right size?" anxiety.
- Walking into a store and being upsold.
- Cleats that last half a season.
- Confusing cleat surface types (FG vs. MG vs. turf vs. indoor).
- Ordering the wrong size jersey number for the team.

## How They Search

- "best cleats for 8 year old"
- "how should cleats fit a kid"
- "youth soccer cleat size chart"
- "shin guard size for 10 year old"
- "ball size u10"
- "cheap soccer cleats that last"
- "what cleats does [pro] wear kids version"

## Content That Would Land With Them

- Sizing charts with photos.
- "First season gear list" articles by age group.
- Honest cleat roundups ("the $60 pick, the $100 pick, the $180 pick").
- "When to size up" advice.
- Video of a parent fitting a cleat on a kid.

## What They Do NOT Care About

- Lifestyle copy.
- Pro player signature stories beyond the 1-line mention.
- Tech spec deep dives on stud configuration.

## Who They Trust

- Other parents at the sideline.
- Their kid's coach.
- Honest reviews with visible testers.
- A local shop with actual people behind the counter (this is a brand advantage for ProSoccer).
