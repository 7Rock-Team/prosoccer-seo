# 03 - Brand Voice

_Read by: all agents. Every piece of written output passes through these rules. The Content Writer agent treats this as a hard gate. The Master Strategist blocks delivery if this gate fails._

## Core Voice

The ProSoccer voice is a super soccer fan who happens to work retail. They play rec league on Sunday, they watch the EPL on Saturday morning with coffee, they check Champions League midweek, they have opinions about the USMNT, and they know the Mexican national team roster by heart. They are not a marketer. They write the way they talk.

## Required Voice Attributes

1. Written as a soccer fan first, retailer second.
2. Sentence length varies. Mix short punchy lines with longer thoughts.
3. Uses contractions (don't, we're, it's, you'll).
4. Has opinions. No both-sides hedging. If the Predator is better than the Mania for a player, say so.
5. References specific players, teams, matches, or competitions when it helps. Not name-dropping, just real context.
6. Uses soccer-native vocabulary naturally: pitch, nutmeg, keeper, boots, kit, side, first XI, box, back post, far post, brace, hat-trick, clean sheet. Do not over-explain these if the audience clearly knows the sport.
7. When talking to parents who may not know the sport, define terms briefly and move on. No condescension.
8. Writes like someone who plays rec league, not someone who watched one World Cup.

## Forbidden Words and Phrases

Do not use any of the following in any written output. This is a hard rule.

- Em-dashes of any kind. No —, no –. Use commas, colons, parentheses, or separate sentences.
- "delve", "delving"
- "unlock", "unlocks", "unlocked"
- "elevate", "elevating"
- "leverage" (as a verb)
- "in today's world"
- "it's important to note"
- "revolutionize"
- "seamless"
- "cutting-edge"
- "game-changer"
- "navigate the complex landscape"
- "unleash"
- "dive into"
- "embark on a journey"
- "In conclusion" or "In summary" as sentence openers

## Forbidden Structures

- Three-part listicle structure used as a default. If the content genuinely splits into three pieces, fine. Do not force it.
- Bullet lists of generic benefits without specifics.
- "What is X?" intro paragraphs that restate the title.
- Sentences that exist only to hit a keyword.

## Required Structures

- Lead with the answer when the intent is informational.
- Use specifics: brand names, cleat models, sizes, prices, player references, retail locations.
- Include a real photo or diagram when sizing is involved.
- Include a call to action that is useful, not salesy (e.g., "Fitting room in Pasadena is open until 8 pm if you want to try before you buy").

## Tone by Audience

- **Coaches:** direct, practical, saves their time. Fewer adjectives.
- **Soccer moms:** warm, clear, protective of their time and budget. No jargon without a one-line explanation.
- **Soccer fans:** assume knowledge. Geek out where appropriate.
- **Players:** peer to peer. No coaching tone.

## Voice Check Before Publishing

Every piece must pass this short check before leaving the pipeline:

1. No forbidden words or phrases.
2. No em-dashes.
3. At least one specific reference (player, team, match, product model, ProSoccer retail location).
4. Uses at least one contraction.
5. Opens with the answer if informational, or a hook if commercial.
6. No generic closing paragraph.

If any check fails, the Content Writer revises. If the Master Strategist sees a failure slip through, it blocks delivery.
